Remembering Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015.

Kalam was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile technology. He played a crucial role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power.

Throughout his life, Kalam emphasized the importance of education and inspired millions of young minds with his vision for a developed India. He was a recipient of several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.

Despite his numerous achievements, Kalam remained humble and approachable, earning him immense respect and admiration from people across the globe.
